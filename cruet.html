olive oil cruet concept + render

timeline: 3 weeks

team: Serena Ho, Sarah Tong

overview

This concept was created as the result of a look into the classics of Italian industrial design. The main source of inspiration that drove the concept was the work of Ettore Sottsass - one of the most prominent designers of the 20th century.

process

My team researched and analyzed the values present in Sottsass’s work and the ways they find reflection in final physical form. Two main values that helped guide our process are attention to material and achieving complexity with the least amount of excess volume. Our product of choice was an olive oil cruet as it is one of the most common objects in traditional Italian households.

Though the typology research we collected a wide array of shapes visible in the design of oil containers across cultures and time periods. It inspired us to look beyond modern oil bottles with standardized metal pourers.

By excluding the metal pourer from the final product form we managed to reinforce a historically traditional look of an oil cruet. It helped achieve an elegant form that is both visually unified and functionally easy to use.

final form

Sottsass’s philosophy of combining complexity and minimalism found its reflection in the smart recombination present in this cruet. We blended an olive oil cruet and a vase into one object that allows to infuse oil with aromatic herbs. This added dimension of complexity introduced conceptual and functional value to the product without making the form excessive.

reflection

This project was my first encounter with the field of industrial design. It taught me to approach everyday objects from a different perspective - thinking about their form, position in space and relationship with the environment and other objects around. Apart from that I had a chance to develop my 3D modeling and rendering skills which were applicable to my consequent personal and academic projects.
